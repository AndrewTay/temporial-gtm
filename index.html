Part I · Top-down — the model 01 · The shift

Motion is the bottleneck the whole 3D economy is stuck behind

First principles: a 3D asset is worthless until it moves with intent. Generation solved the cheap part (static meshes — Meshy, Tripo, Hunyuan, CSM). The expensive part — rigging and animation — is still manual, slow, and locked behind specialist skill. That bottleneck is the most valuable real estate in 3D, because it gates every downstream use. Collapse it and you don't win one app — you unlock the layer beneath all of them.

The Jevons move

When the skill barrier on a content type collapses, the market doesn't redistribute — it grows 10–100×. Canva did it to design, CapCut to video, Roblox to game-making, Midjourney to images. 3D animation has the highest skill wall in all of content and latent demand in every screen and headset. That makes it the single richest democratization left — and the prize is the new market that doesn't exist yet, not a slice of the pro one.

04 · Our right to win

Why us — and why this compounds instead of commoditizing

Be honest about what is not our right to win: static generation. It's commoditizing — the labs and Meshy/Tripo/CSM will give text→3D away. We win the layer above it, on four reinforcing pillars.

1 · Depth in the hardest layer

Motion on arbitrary, non-human topology is where every generalist stops: Mixamo is humanoid-only, Meshy assumes a biped, and Anything World ships bare bones — no faces, clothing, or props, and decimates high-poly. We go deepest exactly where the value is hardest to reach.

2 · The 4D motion-data flywheel

Mocap libraries exist — but paired data of arbitrary non-human meshes → good rigs + motion, from real creator usage, doesn't. Every asset we animate mints one of those pairs: a dataset you earn through distribution, not buy, that compounds the more we're used.

3 · Horizontal embedding

As a motion API / layer, not a vertical app, we embed across games, AR, commerce, and Physical AI — riding everyone's growth via B2B2C. The same engine serves six vectors, so distribution and data reinforce each other.

4 · Speed & good-enough

We productize the just-now-possible before it's perfect, winning jank-tolerant creator communities first while incumbents wait for polish. Distribution + data accrue while others debate quality bars.

The one-line thesis

We win the motion layer because we go deepest on the hardest problem and compound data you earn, not buy, then embed that everywhere. Generation is a race to zero; motion + data is a flywheel. The wedge is copyable for a quarter; the data and the distribution it buys are not.

Part II · Bottom-up — the concrete plan 05 · The wedge

Start narrow on purpose — the on-ramp to the motion layer

A three-person team can't boil a $100B ocean. The wedge has to do four jobs at once: make money now, generate the motion data, build distribution, and sit in a fast-growing vector. That points to one on-ramp: non-human / arbitrary-topology creator assets — and not just VTuber/VRChat.

Wedge selection criteria

  • Arbitrary-topology dense — where our depth beats generalists.
  • Jank-tolerant — good-enough ships; no cinematic bar.
  • Already paying — revealed demand, not a waitlist.
  • Small-team-accessible — global, digital-native, self-serve.
  • Feeds the flywheel — every job mints motion data.

The on-ramp (broadened)

  • VTT tokens — ~100% non-human, idle-loop bar, proven Patreon market. Run first for cash.
  • Creature avatars — VTuber/VRChat and Roblox/Minecraft/Fortnite UGC, not just one app.
  • Indie-dev creatures & props — Unity/Godot/UEFN, Mixamo can't touch them.
  • Mobile-AR characters — Snap/TikTok effect creators, real volume today.
Why broaden beyond VTuber/VRChat

VRChat alone is small (~8.2M registered, a ~350-creator economy) — it can't be the business. But the same engine serves Roblox (tens of millions of daily creators-as-users), Minecraft, indie gamedev, and AR. Same non-human-motion capability, an order of magnitude more demand. The wedge is the capability, not the community.

M01

The build — robust pipeline & product

Product

Goal: alpha → a robust, end-to-end pipeline a non-pro runs unaided. Harden Rung 0–1; instrument the data flywheel from day one.

Validation signals
  • Rig-success ≥ bar, climbing per topology.
  • Cost-to-serve ≤ ~$2–3/asset — margin holds.
  • Non-pro activates without hand-holding.
  • Cleanup time per asset trending down.
Anti-signals
  • Every output needs a manual re-rig.
  • Cost-to-serve 2–3× the assumption.
  • Reliability collapses on the first public drop.
M02

Traction — first paying customers

Revenue

Goal: 100 paying customers (token subs + avatar buyers) or one platform pilot signed — real revenue, not a waitlist. Tokens for cash, avatars for the bet.

Validation signals
  • First dollar within 30 days; MRR compounding.
  • ≥100 paying or one signed pilot.
  • Repeat + organic referral; CAC ≈ free.
  • A creator switches their pipeline to you.
Anti-signals
  • Free trial, no payment — interest ≠ WTP.
  • Activation only with hand-holding.
  • One-and-done — no repeat, no referral.
M03

Capital — raise the seed

Fundraise

Goal: close a seed — YC or top-tier VC — walking in with product and traction, not slides. Build relationships from Day 1; open the round only when M01 + M02 signals are real.

Validation signals
  • Investor second-meetings off the demo + traction.
  • A term sheet, YC interview, or acceptance.
  • Inbound after a public traction moment.
Anti-signals
  • Interest in the vision but not the traction.
  • Process drags >6 weeks, no second meetings.
  • You're pitching slides, not showing product.
Definition of success at Day 100

All three: a robust self-serve product at proven unit economics; 100 paying or one signed pilot with repeat + referral; a seed closed off demo + traction. Miss the build and traction is hollow; miss the traction and the raise is a deck.

09 · What must be true

The honest gates — and what de-risks each

1 · Motion clears the “usable” bar

Rigging is solid; believable looped motion is the hard rung and the 4D path is still heavy. De-risk: sell rig + idle loop now (tokens), scope motion to idle until robust locomotion exists.

2 · The data flywheel actually compounds

The moat only forms if every job improves the model. De-risk: instrument input→output capture from Day 1; measure whether cleanup time falls per cohort.

3 · One lighthouse platform deal lands

L2 is the leverage; it needs a marquee logo by Year 2. De-risk: start B2B2C conversations during the wedge; a signed pilot counts.

4 · Unit economics hold at scale

At 1M+ users, GPU COGS scales. De-risk: measure GPU-seconds/asset in week 0; keep per-asset cost in cents or the margin erodes.

5 · Focus survives the ambition

“Whatever it takes” is where focus dies. De-risk: climb the ladder in order — one paid push at a time; gates before funding the next level.

Why-now

Generation got solved, moving the bottleneck to motion, while six markets demand it at once. The window is the gap between “anyone can make a mesh” and “someone can animate them all.” It won't stay open.
